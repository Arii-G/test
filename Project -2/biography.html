Biography

The life and career of Donald J. Trump

  • Business Career

Early Life and Education

Donald John Trump was born on June 14, 1946, in Queens, New York City. He is the son of Fred Trump, a successful real estate developer, and Mary Anne MacLeod Trump.

He attended the Kew-Forest School, then the New York Military Academy. Trump later studied at Fordham University and graduated from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania in 1968 with a degree in economics.

Business Career

After college, Trump joined his father's firm, E. Trump & Son. He expanded it into The Trump Organization, developing high-profile buildings, hotels, and resorts globally.

Notable projects include Trump Tower, various international Trump hotels, and ventures into entertainment like the Miss Universe pageants and "The Apprentice."

Television Career

Trump hosted NBC’s reality show "The Apprentice" beginning in 2004, which ran for 14 seasons and made the phrase “You’re fired” a cultural staple.

Political Career

Trump announced his candidacy in 2015, winning the 2016 election and serving as the 45th President from 2017 to 2021.

He returned in 2024, won the election against Kamala Harris, and became the 47th President, one of the few to serve non-consecutive terms.

Personal Life

Trump has five children from three marriages: Donald Jr., Ivanka, Eric, Tiffany, and Barron. He has authored books including the bestselling "The Art of the Deal."
